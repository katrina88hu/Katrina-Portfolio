Overview

Designing for the highest-stakes environment imaginable

MoonRanger is a small, fast lunar rover developed at Carnegie Mellon University. Mission Control is the ground interface that operators use to monitor telemetry, issue commands, and respond to anomalies — all with a communication delay and no room for error.

This project required rethinking how operators build situational awareness under cognitive load, time pressure, and extreme uncertainty.

The Challenge

How might we help operators maintain control when the stakes are absolute?

How might we design a ground control interface that helps a small team make fast, confident decisions in a high-latency, mission-critical environment?

  • 1

    Communication delay means commands can't be undone once sent.

  • 2

    Dense telemetry floods operators with data — signal must be separated from noise.

  • 3

    Small team means one operator must hold multiple mental models simultaneously.
